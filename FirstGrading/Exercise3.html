How to Get a Barangay Clearance

A Barangay Clearance is a government-issued document in the Philippines. It is used to certify that a certain person is of good moral character and a law-abiding citizen in that barangay. Getting a Barangay Clearance is easy and fast that it can be done in 30 minutes or less.

This document is a one of the common requirements in processing legal documents and is also used in financial transactions in the locality. This document is required for Local Employments, Government Agency Transactions, Travel Abroads, Police Clearance, NBI Clearance, Loan Application, Scholarship Application and others that might need it.

Barangay Clearance Requirements and other Things to Prepare

  • Recent Cedula (This is your Community Tax Certificate)
  • Cash for the Barangay Clearance Fee
  • Application Form (This is issued by your barangay hall)
  • Black Ballpen

It is possible to get your recent cedula at your nearest municapal hall if it is not issued by your barangay hall for any kind of reason. The Barangay Clearance Fee varies in every barangay but usually it should not be less than P100.00.

How to Get a Barangay Clearance: Steps to Follow

  1. After acquiring and preparing requirements, go to your barangay hall.

    2. Some tips before going to your barangay hall.

    • Be tidy. You are applying for a legal document so atleast dressed appropriately.
    • Prepare enough cash to pay for unexpected fees. This will save you time.

  2. Ask the Barangay Secretary or assigned officer for the task that you want to request for a Barangay Clearance. For security and clarifications, you might be asked a few questions such as the purpose of your request, address, how long have you been a resident of that barangay and other questions that will help certify that you will not use the clearance for misdeeds and authenticate that you are their resident.

    3. Some tips when you are in the barangay hall.

    • Be polite in conversing. Respect begets respect.

  3. You will be given an application form that you will fill out and submit to the assigned officer.

    4. Some tips on filling out your application form.

    • Write legibly on the application form.

  4. Pay for the Barangay Clearance Fee to the treasurer.
  5. The office will check your backgroung before processing your request. If your name is clear of any bad records, your Barangay Clearance will be printed and you'll be asked to put a thumbprint and a signature.
  6. Wait for your clearance to be signe by the Barangay Captain and make sure it is stamped and sealed with the Barangay Official Seal.
  7. Claim your fresh Barangay Clearance after a few minutes.

    8. Some tips after claiming your clearance.

    • Secure your clearance in a clean envelop to protect it from elements and always keep it clean and uncrumpled.

Your Barangay Clearance should contain your name, address, request purpose, date issued, thumbprint, and signature. Make sure it has a signature of the Barangay Captain, stamped and sealed with the Barangay Official Seal to make it valid.

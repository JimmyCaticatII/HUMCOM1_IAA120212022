Who are the Dragonborns?

Dragonborn, also referred to as Dovahkiin in the Dragon Language, are individuals with the body of a mortal, but the blood and soul of a Dragon. Some believe that a Dragonborn appears on the world in times of great need by the command of the Gods.

Cultural Beliefs

"Dragons have the inborn ability to learn and project the their Voice. Dragons also are able to absorb the power of their slain brethren. A few mortals are born with similar abilities, wether a gift or a curse has been a matter of debate through centuries. What you have already learned in a few days took even the most gifted of us years to achieve. Some beleive that Dragonborns are sent into the world by the gods, at times of great need." -Arngeir, Leader of the Greybeards

By Nord tradition, a Dragonborn represents what a Nord should strive to be. The Nords believe a Dragonborn represent the end of all Skyrim's foes. Nord tales likewise tell of Dragonborn of old who were great and powerful Dragonslayers, a beleif which the Blades of the late Fourth Era also believe to be the reason why the old Akaviri Dragonguard came to serve the Dragonborn.

It is unknown as to how a Dragonborn is chosen. The various dynasties of Dragonborn Emperors led to a common beleif that the Dragon blood is hereditary, though since it is a blessing of Akatosh himself it cannot be known how or why it is bestowed. It is unkown if the Dragonborn Emperors were Dragonborn, and thus able to wear the Amulet of Kings and light the Dragonfires, or if these acts were the sign of Akatosh's blessings for these Dragonborns.

The Greybeards

After slaying a Dragon, and learning the meaning of a Word of Power, the Greybeards are able to sense this and summon the Dragonborn. Should a Dragonborn choose to answer the summons of the Greybeards, they are first tested to see if they truly posses the Thu'um. A Dragonborn has the inborn ability to both learn and master a new Word of Power far quicker than a normal mortal can, as regular mortals usually need years of training to learn a Word of Power. Due to the inborn ability of Dragonborns to use the Thu'umm the Greybeards seek to guide them in the proper usage of their power.

In order to truly show a Dragonborn's abilities, the Greybeards send them off to retrieve the Horn of Jurgen Windcaller, the found of the Greybeards. Upon retrieving the Horn and returning it, the Greybeards formally name the Dragonborn "Ysmir", the "Dragon of the North", to declare the Dragonborn has accepted their guidance.

History

Merethic Era

Miraak, who lived during the Dragon War of the Merethic Era, became known as the First Dragonborn. The Three Tongues, Hakon One-Eye, Gormliath Golden-Hilt, and Felldir the Old, who led the charge against Alduin had asked Miraak for his aid, though Miraak denied, having other plans. Miraak would eventually be defeated in combat by Vahlok the Jailor, though saved by Hermaeus Mora.00000

First Era

During the First Era, the Nedic Slave rebellion in Cyrodiil against the Ayleids would take place, being led by Slave Queen Alessia. Alessia, being a Dragonborn, was gifted the Amulet of Kings by Akatosh, which would be used to light the Dragonfires and keep the armies of Oblivion at bay. Alessia's Line would continue with more Dragonborn Emperors and Empresses, until their deaths.

In 1E 2703, the Akaviri attempted an invasion of Tamriel, which would eventually be foiled by the efforts of Reman Cyrodiil. The exact nature of defeat of the Akaviri is unknown, though it is known that they were not seeking for combat with Reman, but were instead searching for him, as he was a Dragonborn. Under Reman Cyrodiil, the Dragon blood officially became part of becoming a legitimate Emperor, as he included the rituals gears to the Amulet of Kings, and the lighting of the Dragonfires, in the rites of becoming and Emperor. His dysnasty would continue until the death of Juliek Cyrodiil during the Four Score War.

Second Era

As of 2E 579, it had been years since the last Dragonborn Emperor had ruled. Following Varen Aquilarios successful rebellion against the Longhouse Emperors, he aimed to legitimize his rule through lighting the Dragonfires. Varen was not a Dragonborn, and was convinced by Mannimarco to participate in the ritual which would end up being a trick. Instead of becoming a Dragonborn, Varen and his companions were forced to the ground, while Mannimarco corrupted the power of the Amulet of Kings to trigger the Soulburst, and eventually Planemeld, until it was stopped by the Vestige.

After Kaalgrontiid and his kin were freed from the Halls of Colossus, one such dragon named Sahtronax was captured by the Hollowfang Clan of vampires with the assistance of the rogue Dragonguard Grundwulf, who then proceeded to drink his blood believing it would make him a Dragonborn and with that power unite the fractured Empire once more. Though the ritual granted him extraordinary abilities, he was slain shortly afterward.

Years later, in 2E 828m another Dragonborn would be placed on the world. This Dragonborn being Tiber Septim. Tiber Septim would learn the Thu'um from Wulfharth, and meet the Greybeards shortly after, who told him he was destined to unite Tamriel. During his campaigns in Cyrodiil, he would find the Amulet of Kings during the Battle of Sancre Tor against the Nord-Breton Alliance. After having become Emperor, Tiber Septim declared the start of the Third Era.

Third Era

Tiber Septim's dynas of Dragonborn Emperors would continue to rule the Third Empire until the Oblivion Crisis. Under the reign of Emperor Uriel Septim VII, the Mythic Dawn attempted to slay the Dragonborn Emperors and thus provide Mehrunes Dagon with the means to cross over into Tamriel. Uriel Septim and his three sons were assassinated, with only his bastard son, Martin Septim having survived. The leader of the Mythic Dawn cult, Mankar Camoran, was likewises a Dragonborn, able to speak fire and wear the Amulet of Kings. Mankar would eventually be slain in his Paradise, whereas Martin Septim would meet his end after he destroyed the Amulet of Kings in order to banish Mehrunes Dagon.

Fourth Era

During the 4E 201, the prophecy of the Last Dragonborn would come to pass. Alduin, who had been banished from Tamriel since the Merethic Era by an Elder Scroll, returned to the land, indirectly helping the Last Dragonborn to escape an Imperial execution at Helgen by destroying the town. The Last Dragonborn would learn of his nature while aiding the city of Whiterun by slaying the Dragon Mirmulnir at the Western Watchtower of Whiterun. Upon defeating the Dragon, the Dragonborn gained its soul, and through the Word of Power that the Dragonborn had learned previously in a mission of the court wizard, was able to use the Thu'um.

After learning Dragonrend, the Last Dragonborn was able to face off against Alduin to atop the Throat of the World, winning a minor victory against the World-Eater, though Alduin did escape to Sovngarde. The Dragonborn would give chase, and through the help of Odahviing, they would gain access to Skuldafn and its portal to Sovngarde. In Sovngarde, the Last Dragonborn would meet up with the three Nord Tongues of Old, Gormliath, Hakon, and Felldir, and together they faced off against Alduin, defeating him in combat. It is unknown if Alduin was truly slain, as in contrast to regular Dragons, his soul was not absorbed. The leader of the Greybeards, Arngeir, believed that it may have been possible that he could return to fulfill his true destiny, though that was for the gods to decide.

The First Dragonborn, Miraak, had also gained more power since his defeat by Vahlok. Upon learning of the Last Dragonborn, he had sent out cultists with the orders to defeat the Dragonborn. The Last Dragonborn would travel to Solstheim, where the cultists had originated, in order to find the origin of the cultists. Here, he would discover that Miraak had corrupted the All-Maker Stones in order to increase his power for his planned return. During his quest to stop Miraak, Miraak held the power to transport the Last Dragonborn every time he would slew a Dragon, and steal the Dragon's soul before the Last Dragonborn could absorb it. With the aid of Hermaeus Mora, the Last Dragonborn would learn the necessary powers required to defeat Miraak, primarily the Bend Will Thu'um. The Last Dragonborn would travel to Apocrypha one last time to face Miraak in combat. After a long battle, Hermaeus Mora ended the conflict by slaying Miraak for his treachery, and naming the Last Dragonborn his champion. This was because Hermaeus Mora did not desire Miraak to be set free from his direct control, and instead wanted a more loyal servant.

Notable Dragonborns

  • Miraak, the First Dragonborn, who lived during the Merethic Era and died in the Fourth Era following a fight with the Last Dragonborn.
  • Saint Alessia, the First Dragonborn Empress, who lived during the first Era and would rule the Alessian Empire until her death in 1E 266
  • Wulfharthm a Nord Dragonborn who fought against the Direnni and the Alessian Order until his death in 1E 533.
  • Reman Cyrodiil, an Imperial Dragonborn, who would become the emperor of the Reman Empire and adopt the Akaviri Dragonguard into his private bodyguard. He died peacefully in 1E 2762.
  • Tiber Septim, a human Dragonborn, who would become the first emperor to unite all of Tamriel under the Septim Empire. Tiber Septim died peacefully in the year 3E 38.
  • Mankar Camoran, an Altmer Dragonborn, who attempted to give Mehrunes Dagon domination over Tamriel. He was slain in Paradise by the Hero of Kvatch in 3E 433.
  • Martin Septim, an Imperial Dragonborn and the last of the Septim dysnasty of emperors. He sacrificed himself and the Amulet of Kings in order to beat beack Mehrunes Dagon and permanently seal the gates to Oblivion in 3E 433.
  • Last Dragonborn, the Dragonborn prophesied on Alduin's Wall by the Akaviri, and in the Elder Scrolls, who was destined to defeat Alduin.
